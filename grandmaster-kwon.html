Grandmaster Kwon, Jae-Hwa · A lifetime devoted to Traditional Taekwon-Do

The source of our tradition

Technique, discipline and character

Grandmaster Kwon, Jae-Hwa played a defining role in establishing and spreading Traditional Taekwon-Do in Europe.

Born in Busan, South Korea, in 1937, he began training as a young man and later became an influential teacher, author and international ambassador of the martial art. His teaching joined precise technique with perseverance, self-control and responsibility. These principles continue to guide the training at Traditional Taekwon-Do Kutaisi.

“A martial art is not mastered in a moment. Its value lies in continually giving your best and working on yourself.”

A guiding principle associated with the teaching of Grandmaster Kwon, Jae-Hwa

Selected milestones

A life dedicated to Taekwon-Do

1937

Born in Busan, South Korea

The beginning of a life that would influence Traditional Taekwon-Do far beyond Korea.

1965

Taekwon-Do comes to Europe

Member of the selected Korean demonstration team that introduced the martial art across Europe and other regions.

1966–1972

Building Taekwon-Do in Germany

Kai Hacker's personal journey

Black belts earned under the Grandmaster

Journey began · 2000

Kai Hacker began training in Traditional Taekwon-Do. Nine years of consistent practice, discipline and personal development led to his first black belt examination.

1st Dan · 15.11.2009

Black belt examination in Gräfelfing near Munich under the personal direction of Grandmaster Kwon, Jae-Hwa.

2nd Dan · 21.02.2012

Awarded during an intensive training and examination journey in Portland, Oregon.

17–25 February 2012 · Portland, Oregon

Nine days that tested everything

The 2nd Dan was not decided in a single examination. It was earned through continuous evaluation across the entire training journey.

Grandmaster Kwon invited a delegation from Germany to his private dojang in Portland. Every day Kai had to demonstrate technique, physical endurance, concentration and mental strength. The examination included continuous breaking tests involving a total of 40 wooden boards. On 21 February 2012, Grandmaster Kwon personally awarded Kai the 2nd Dan.
